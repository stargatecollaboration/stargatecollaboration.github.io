Dr Hannah R. Wakeford

 

Title: Giacconi Fellow

Institution: STScI

Department: Science Mission Office

Email: hwakeford at stsci.edu

Research Interests: exoplanet clouds; giant exoplanets; spectroscopy; observations

Hannah is the 2017 Giacconi Fellow at STScI. Her work focuses on characterizing the atmospheres of exoplanets through observations with the Hubble Space Telescope, and working towards a better understanding of how exoplanets can be explored further with Webb. Hannah has also developed theoretical models for exotic clouds in exoplanet atmosphere and uses them to interpret observations and make prediction for future studies.

Dr Kevin B. Stevenson

 

Title: Research Astronomer

Institution: STScI

Department: Instrument Devision

Email: kbs at stsci.edu

Research Interests: transiting exoplanets, atmospheric characterization, mission development

Kevin is an ESA/AURA Astronomer, interested in characterizing the architectures and atmospheres of extra-solar planets and developing a classification scheme to better understand their nature and origin. To achieve these goals, Kevin uses ground- and space-based telescopes to measure the composition and chemical properties of transiting exoplanets as they pass in front of or behind their parent stars.

Sarah E. Moran

 

Title: Graduate Research Assistant

Institution: Johns Hopkins University

Department: Earth- and Planetary Sciences

Email: smoran14 at jhu.edu

Research Interests: aerosol formation, atmospheric evolution, clouds, hazes

Sarah is a third year graduate student at Johns Hopkins working on a combination of atmospheric models and laboratory experiments to understand aerosol formation on exoplanets. She’s interested in the effects of such aerosols on the temperature structure, radiative transfer, and ultimately on the habitability of new worlds. She’s also very interested in science communication and science policy. Sarah is currently a NASA Earth and Space Science Fellow in Astrophysics.

Dr Jonathan Fraine

 

Title: Research Scientist

Institution: Space Science Institute

Department: Center for Extra-solar planetary systems

Email: jfraine@spacescience.org

Research Interests: Exoplanet Atmospheric Observation, Transiting Exoplanet Detection, Time Series Observations, Machine and Deep Learning, Jame Webb Space Telescope Calibration

Jonatahn is a Research Scientist at the Space Sciences Institute. His observational projects detail characterizing exoplanetary atmospheres and general light curve analyses: include detecting supernovae and exoplanets with Kepler, K2, TESS. He also uses artificial intelligence (e.g. machine & deep learning) to detect anomalies in Hubble and James Webb (future) observations. He uses both machine learning classification, regression, and clustering to identify and mitigate systematic noise sources with Spitzer Space Telescope and James Webb Space Telescope calibration observations.
